## 2. Getting Started

### Accessing the System

**Web Access:**
- URL: [https://your-hospital.management-system.com]
- Works on any modern browser: Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge
- Accessible from hospital computers, tablets, or your phone

1. Navigate to the system URL

**Your Dashboard:**

### Navigation

**Main Menu (Left Sidebar):**
- Home/Dashboard

**Notifications:**

## 3. For Doctors: Consultation Workflow

### Your Patient Queue

**Accessing the Queue:**
1. From dashboard, click "Consultations" or "Patient Queue"
2. You'll see all patients currently waiting for you

**Queue Display:**

| Patient | Priority | Wait Time | Status | Actions |
|---------|----------|-----------|--------|---------|
| John Doe (PAT-2024-0123) | Emergency | 15 min | Waiting | Start Consultation |
| Jane Smith (PAT-2024-0124) | Urgent | 32 min | Waiting | Start Consultation |
| Bob Johnson (PAT-2024-0125) | Normal | 45 min | Waiting | Start Consultation |

**Priority Levels:**
- **Emergency** (Red) - Life-threatening, see immediately
- **Urgent** (Orange) - Serious condition, see within 30 min
- **Normal** (Green) - Routine consultation

### Starting a Consultation

**Step 1: Select Patient**
- Click "Start Consultation" button next to patient name
- Patient automatically moved to "In Progress" status
- Other departments see patient is currently with doctor

**Step 2: Review Patient History**

The consultation screen shows:

**Left Panel - Patient Information:**
- Demographics (name, age, gender)
- Allergies (prominently highlighted in red)
- Current medications
- Chronic conditions/problems
- Insurance information

**Center Panel - Current Visit:**
- Chief complaint (from reception)
- Vitals (latest measurements)
- Current consultation notes
- Prescription section
- Test orders section

**Right Panel - History:**
- Previous visits
- Past prescriptions
- Previous lab/radiology results
- Problem list

### Recording Vitals

If vitals not already recorded:

1. Click "Record Vitals" button
2. Enter measurements:
   - Blood Pressure (systolic/diastolic)
   - Temperature
   - Pulse rate
   - Respiratory rate
   - Oxygen saturation
   - Weight
3. Click "Save Vitals"

**Note:** Abnormal values highlighted in red automatically

### Consultation Notes

1. Click in "Consultation Notes" section
2. Document:
   - Subjective (patient's complaint)
   - Objective (your findings)
   - Assessment (diagnosis)
   - Plan (treatment plan)
3. Notes save automatically as you type
4. You can use templates for common conditions (click "Templates")

### Writing Prescriptions

**Step 1: Add Medication**
1. Click "Add Prescription" button
2. Search for medication name
3. Select from dropdown

**Step 2: Enter Details**
- Dosage: e.g., "500mg"
- Frequency: Select from dropdown (Once daily, Twice daily, etc.)
- Route: Oral, IV, IM, etc.
- Duration: e.g., "7 days" or "14 days"
- Quantity: Total tablets/vials
- Instructions: "Take with food", "Before bedtime", etc.

**Step 3: Check Alerts**
- System shows allergy warnings in RED if applicable
- Drug interaction warnings (if configured)
- Duplicate medication alerts

**Step 4: Save**
- Click "Add to Prescription"
- Prescription instantly sent to pharmacy
- Pharmacist receives notification

**Multiple Medications:**
- Repeat process for each medication
- All medications on one prescription
- Click "Finalize Prescription" when done

### Ordering Lab Tests

**Step 1: Order Test**
1. Click "Order Lab Test" button
2. Search for test name (e.g., "CBC", "Lipid Panel")
3. Select test from dropdown

**Step 2: Add Details**
- Priority: Normal or Urgent
- Instructions: Special requirements
- Clinical notes: Reason for test

**Step 3: Submit**
- Click "Order Test"
- Lab instantly receives order
- Lab tech will collect sample and process

**Step 4: Get Results**
- You'll be notified when results ready
- **Critical results** trigger instant alert
- View results in patient's "Lab Results" tab

### Ordering Radiology/Imaging

**Same process as lab tests:**
1. Click "Order Radiology Test"
2. Select test type (X-ray, CT, MRI, Ultrasound)
3. Specify body part
4. Add clinical indication
5. Submit order

### Completing Consultation

**When finished:**
1. Review all prescriptions and orders
2. Click "Complete Consultation"
3. Select patient's next step:
   - Discharge (patient can leave)
   - Admit (for inpatient care)
   - Refer (to specialist)
4. Patient automatically removed from your queue
5. If prescriptions ordered, pharmacy notified
6. If tests ordered, lab/radiology notified
7. Billing automatically receives charge information

### Handling Critical Alerts

**When critical lab result received:**

1. **Pop-up notification** appears on your screen
2. **Audible alert** (if enabled)
3. **Email/SMS** sent to your contact info

**Alert shows:**
- Patient name and ID
- Test name
- Critical value
- Normal range
- Patient's location

**Actions:**
1. Click notification to view full result
2. Review result and patient history
3. **Acknowledge alert** (required within 15 min)
4. Take clinical action (call patient, adjust treatment, etc.)
5. Document your action in consultation notes

**Escalation:**
- If not acknowledged in 15 min, alert escalates to department head
- Compliance tracking for all critical results

## 5. For Receptionists: Registration & Check-In

### Registering New Patients

**Step 1: Start Registration**
1. From dashboard, click "Register New Patient"
2. New patient form opens

**Step 2: Enter Demographics**

*Required fields:*
- First Name, Last Name
- Date of Birth
- Gender
- Phone Number
- Email (if available)

*Additional fields:*
- Address
- Emergency Contact (name, relationship, phone)
- Insurance Information
- ID Number (driver's license, passport)
- Marital Status, Occupation
- Blood Group (if known)

**Patient ID auto-generated:**
- Format: PAT-2024-XXXX
- Unique identifier for patient
- Used in all future interactions

**Step 3: Save Patient**
- Click "Register Patient"
- Patient added to database
- You'll see confirmation with patient ID

### Checking In Existing Patients

**For appointments or walk-ins:**

**Step 1: Find Patient**
- Click "Check-In Patient"
- Search by:
  - Patient ID (PAT-2024-XXXX)
  - Phone number
  - Name (first or last)

**Step 2: Verify Patient**
- Confirm patient details on screen match patient in front of you
- Update any changed information (phone, address, insurance)

**Step 3: Record Visit Details**
- Reason for visit/Chief Complaint
- Department (General, Emergency, etc.)
- Doctor preference (if any)
- Visit type (New consultation, Follow-up)

**Step 4: Complete Check-In**
- Click "Check-In Patient"
- New visit created
- Patient added to doctor's queue automatically
- Priority set based on visit type (Emergency → Urgent → Normal)

**Patient receives:**
- Visit ID number
- Estimated wait time
- Queue position

**Doctor receives:**
- Notification of new patient in queue
- Can see patient's chief complaint

### Managing Appointments

**Scheduling Appointment:**
1. Click "New Appointment"
2. Search and select patient (or register new)
3. Select doctor
4. Choose date and time
5. Select visit type
6. Add notes
7. Click "Book Appointment"

**Viewing Appointments:**
- Dashboard shows today's appointments
- Click "Appointments" to see full calendar
- Filter by doctor, date, status

**Check-in from Appointment:**
- When patient arrives, find in appointment list
- Click "Check-In" button
- Verify details
- Patient moved to doctor's queue

### Queue Management

**Real-time queue view:**
1. Click "Queue Status"
2. See all patients currently in queue
3. Organized by:
   - Department
   - Doctor
   - Priority level
   - Wait time

**Useful for:**
- Telling patients estimated wait time
- Managing patient expectations
- Seeing bottlenecks

## 6. For Lab Technicians: Laboratory Operations

### Your Work Queue

**Test Order Queue:**
1. From dashboard, click "Lab Tests" or "Pending Tests"
2. See all ordered tests waiting for processing

**Queue shows:**
- Patient name and ID
- Test ordered
- Ordering doctor
- Priority (Normal or Urgent)
- Order time
- Current status

**Statuses:**
- **Pending** - Order received, sample not collected
- **Sample Collected** - Awaiting processing
- **In Progress** - Currently testing
- **Completed** - Results entered, awaiting approval
- **Approved** - Results released to doctor

### Processing Test Orders

**Step 1: Collect Sample**
1. Find test in "Pending" list
2. Click test to open details
3. Verify patient identity
4. Collect sample
5. Click "Mark Sample Collected"
6. Status changes to "Sample Collected"

**Step 2: Process Test**
1. When ready to run test, click test in queue
2. Click "Start Processing"
3. Status changes to "In Progress"
4. Run test on equipment
5. Obtain results

**Step 3: Enter Results**
1. Click test (still showing "In Progress")
2. Click "Enter Results"
3. Enter result values:
   - Use fields provided based on test type
   - Enter numeric values, select from dropdowns, etc.
4. Add technician notes if needed
5. Click "Save Results"

**Step 4: Review & Approve**
- Results marked "Completed" but not yet released
- Senior tech or pathologist reviews
- Click "Approve Results"
- Results instantly distributed

### Critical Result Handling

**When entering critical value:**

1. System automatically detects based on configured ranges
2. **Red warning** appears: "CRITICAL RESULT DETECTED"
3. Confirm result is correct
4. Add any comments
5. Click "Save Results"

**System automatically:**
- Sends instant notification to ordering doctor
- Flags result as critical in patient chart
- Tracks notification delivery
- Creates audit trail

**You don't need to:**
- Call doctor (system does it instantly)
- Track down doctor (multi-channel notifications)
- Document notification (automatic)

**Your role:**
- Enter accurate result
- Double-check critical values
- Add clinical context if needed

### Quality Control

**Recording QC:**
1. Navigate to "Quality Control" section
2. Select test type
3. Enter control values (Level 1, Level 2, etc.)
4. System validates within acceptable range
5. Out-of-range values flagged
6. Document corrective action if needed

**QC tracking:**
- Historical QC data accessible
- Trends and charts available
- Compliance reporting built-in

## 7. For Pharmacists: Medication Dispensing

### Your Prescription Queue

**Viewing Prescriptions:**
1. From dashboard, click "Prescriptions" or "Pharmacy Queue"
2. See all prescriptions waiting to be filled

**Queue shows:**
- Patient name and ID
- Prescribing doctor
- Number of medications
- Prescription time
- Priority
- Status

**Statuses:**
- **Pending** - New prescription, not yet processed
- **In Progress** - Currently being filled
- **Dispensed** - Given to patient
- **Cancelled** - Prescription cancelled

### Filling Prescriptions

**Step 1: Select Prescription**
1. Click prescription from queue
2. Prescription details open

**View:**
- Patient information
- Allergies (highlighted)
- All medications on prescription
- Dosage, frequency, duration for each
- Doctor's instructions

**Step 2: Start Filling**
1. Click "Start Filling"
2. Status changes to "In Progress"
3. For each medication:
   - Locate in inventory
   - Verify medication, strength, form
   - Count/measure quantity
   - Label container
   - Package medication

**Step 3: Check Inventory**
- System shows current stock for each medication
- If stock low, yellow warning appears
- If out of stock, red alert and can't dispense

**Step 4: Dispense to Patient**
1. Verify patient identity (ID, name, DOB)
2. Counsel patient on each medication:
   - How to take
   - Side effects
   - Precautions
3. Click "Dispense" button
4. Select payment method (if applicable):
   - Insurance
   - Cash
   - Patient account
5. Click "Complete Dispensing"

**System automatically:**
- Deducts quantities from inventory
- Creates billing entry
- Notifies billing department
- Updates patient's medication history
- Marks prescription as "Dispensed"

### Inventory Management

**Checking Stock Levels:**
1. Click "Inventory" from main menu
2. See all medications with current quantities

**Stock table shows:**
- Medication name
- Current quantity
- Reorder level
- Status (Good, Low, Out of Stock)

**Low Stock Alerts:**
- Automatic notifications when stock falls below reorder level
- Yellow badge on dashboard
- Medication highlighted in inventory list

**Recording Stock Receipt:**
1. Navigate to "Inventory"
2. Find medication
3. Click "Receive Stock"
4. Enter:
   - Quantity received
   - Batch number
   - Expiry date
   - Supplier
5. Click "Save"
6. Quantity automatically updated

**Stock Adjustments:**
- For damaged, expired, or stolen stock
- Click "Adjust Stock"
- Enter new quantity and reason
- Submit adjustment
- Audit trail maintained

### Handling Alerts

**Drug Allergy Alerts:**
- If prescription contains medication patient is allergic to
- **RED ALERT** appears when viewing prescription
- Shows allergen and medication
- Contact doctor before dispensing
- Can document intervention

**Drug Interactions:** (if configured)
- System checks for interactions between prescribed medications
- Warning displayed if interaction found
- Clinical significance indicated
- Contact doctor if needed

## 8. For Billing Staff: Financial Operations

### Your Dashboard

**Shows:**
- Unbilled services (procedures, tests, medications)
- Pending bills (generated but not paid)
- Today's payments
- Outstanding balances
- Key metrics

### Automatic Charge Capture

**How charges are captured:**

| Activity | Auto-Captured | Source |
|----------|---------------|--------|
| **Consultation** | Doctor fee | When doctor completes consultation |
| **Medications** | Medication cost | When pharmacy dispenses |
| **Lab Tests** | Test charges | When test results approved |
| **Radiology** | Imaging charges | When study completed |
| **Procedures** | Procedure fees | When documented in system |

**Result:** 80% of charges automatically captured without manual entry!

### Viewing Unbilled Services

**Check what needs billing:**
1. Click "Unbilled Services" from dashboard
2. See list of completed services not yet billed

**For each service:**
- Patient name and ID
- Service type (consultation, lab, medication)
- Service date
- Amount
- Status

**Creating Bill:**
1. Select patient
2. Review all unbilled services for that patient
3. Verify accuracy
4. Click "Generate Bill"
5. Bill automatically created
6. Status changes to "Billed - Pending Payment"

### Processing Payments

**When patient pays:**

**Step 1: Find Bill**
- Search by patient ID, name, or bill number
- Click bill from list

**Step 2: Record Payment**
1. Click "Receive Payment"
2. Enter amount paid
3. Select payment method:
   - Cash
   - Card (Debit/Credit)
   - Insurance
   - Bank Transfer
   - Other
4. Add payment reference (check #, transaction ID, etc.)
5. Click "Submit Payment"

**Step 3: Print Receipt**
- Receipt automatically generated
- Print and give to patient
- Electronic copy emailed to patient (if email on file)

**Partial Payments:**
- Enter amount less than total
- Remaining balance tracked
- Payment history visible
- Future payments applied to same bill

### Managing Insurance Claims

**For insured patients:**

**Step 1: Verify Coverage**
- Insurance information from patient registration
- Verify coverage before generating bill
- Note co-pay, deductible, coverage percentage

**Step 2: Generate Insurance Claim**
1. Create bill as normal
2. Select "Insurance" as payment method
3. System generates claim form
4. Review claim details
5. Click "Submit to Insurance"

**Step 3: Track Claim**
- Claim status tracked in system
- Submitted, Under Review, Approved, Denied
- Follow up on pending claims
- Re-submit denied claims after correction

**Step 4: Process Insurance Payment**
- When insurance pays
- Record payment against bill
- Patient billed for remaining balance (co-pay, deductible)

### Outstanding Payments

**Track unpaid bills:**
1. Click "Outstanding Payments"
2. See all bills with balance

**Aging report:**
- 0-30 days
- 31-60 days
- 61-90 days
- 90+ days

**Collections:**
- Filter by age
- Send payment reminders
- Escalate to collections if needed
- Document all communication

## 9. Common Tasks & Quick Reference

### Searching for Patients

**Multiple ways to search:**

**Method 1: Quick Search (Top Bar)**
- Click search box (magnifying glass icon)
- Type patient ID, name, or phone
- Results appear as you type
- Click patient to open chart

**Method 2: Patient List**
- Click "Patients" from main menu
- Use filters: Date range, department, doctor, status
- Sort by name, ID, date
- Click patient to view details

**Search Tips:**
- Patient ID is fastest: PAT-2024-XXXX
- Phone number: Type digits only
- Name: First or last name works
- Partial names work (e.g., "John" finds "Johnson")

### Viewing Patient History

**Open patient chart:**
1. Search and click patient
2. Patient summary opens

**Tabs available:**
- **Overview:** Demographics, allergies, current meds
- **Visits:** All visits with dates and doctors
- **Vitals:** Vital signs history with graphs
- **Prescriptions:** All prescriptions ever written
- **Lab Results:** All lab results with trends
- **Radiology:** Imaging studies
- **Billing:** Financial history
- **Documents:** Uploaded files, scanned docs

**Quick navigation:**
- Use tabs to switch between sections
- Click visit to see details from that visit
- Click lab result to see full report
- Click prescription to see medications from that time

### Printing & Reports

**Printing prescriptions:**
- From consultation screen, click "Print Prescription"
- Formatted for patient
- Includes doctor signature and license number

**Printing bills/receipts:**
- From billing screen, click "Print Receipt"

- Patient can use for insurance reimbursement

**Generating reports:**
- From "Reports" menu (if you have access)

- Choose date range
- Add filters
- Click "Generate"

### Managing Your Profile

**Update your information:**
1. Click your name (top right)

3. Update:
   - Contact information (phone, email)
   - Notification preferences
   - Password
4. Click "Save Changes"

**Notification preferences:**
- Email notifications: On/Off

**Changing password:**
1. Profile → "Change Password"
2. Enter current password
3. Enter new password (12+ chars, mixed case, number, symbol)
4. Confirm new password
5. Click "Update Password"

## 10. Tips & Best Practices

### General Tips

**Accuracy First:**
- Double-check patient identity before any entry
- Verify allergies before prescribing
- Confirm critical values before saving

**Work the Queue:**
- Prioritize Emergency > Urgent > Normal
- Clear oldest items first within same priority
- Don't leave tasks "in progress" when taking break

**Communication:**
- Add clear notes for next person in workflow
- Document all patient interactions
- Use system notifications instead of phone calls when possible

**Security:**
- Lock your screen when stepping away (Windows: Win+L, Mac: Cmd+Ctrl+Q)
- Never share your password
- Log out at end of shift
- Don't let others use your login

### For Better Efficiency

**Keyboard Shortcuts:** (if implemented)
- Ctrl+K: Quick search
- Ctrl+N: New patient/record
- Ctrl+S: Save
- Esc: Close dialog/cancel
- Tab: Move to next field

**Templates:**
- Use consultation note templates for common conditions
- Create medication favorites for frequently prescribed
- Save search filters you use often

**Batch Similar Tasks:**
- Check all notifications at once
- Fill multiple prescriptions in sequence
- Enter multiple lab results together

### Data Quality

**Complete Information:**
- Fill all required fields
- Add additional details when relevant
- Update changed information (phone, address, insurance)

**Standardized Entries:**
- Use consistent formats
- Select from dropdowns when available
- Follow hospital naming conventions

**Timely Documentation:**
- Document as soon as possible
- Don't wait until end of shift
- Real-time entry ensures accuracy
